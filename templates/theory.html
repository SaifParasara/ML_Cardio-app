The Science Behind The AI

Understanding how our Logistic Regression model predicts heart disease.

The Sigmoid Activation

At the core of our prediction engine is the Sigmoid Function. This mathematical curve maps any input value (derived from your health metrics) to a probability between 0 and 1.

$$ P(y=1|x) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-z}} $$

Where $z$ is the weighted sum of your inputs ($w \cdot x + b$)

If the resulting probability $P$ is greater than 0.5 (or a custom threshold for higher recall), the model classifies the risk as "High".

Bias-Variance Tradeoff

Our Logistic Regression model is designed as a high-bias, low-variance classifier. This makes it robust against overfitting, meaning it generalizes well to new patients rather than memorizing noise in the training data.

Feature Importance

The model assigns different "weights" to each factor. Clinical analysis shows that Systolic Blood Pressure and Age carry the highest coefficients, significantly influencing the final risk score.
